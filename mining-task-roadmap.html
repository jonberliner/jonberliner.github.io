Mining Task research track roadmap:

(all are variations on mining task)

Bayesian Optimization Experiment:

distinguishing design features:

  • 125 rounds
  • start w 2/6 observations
  • explore/exploit for 2/6 more active samples (how many unknown to user)
  • no sample cost
  • user receives reward equivalent to deepest sample discovered
  • drilling cost is 50 (is 30 for all other experiments)

point of experiment:

  • introduce ideas (GPs and acquisition functions (AFs)) of Bayesian Optimization (BO; lol) to readers
  • get people familiar with the mining task, and how can yield insight beyond fitting scores
  • show that AFs from BO can explain decision making for single shot decisions (as opposed to repeated decisions) with a single model rather than explore/exploit weighted hibrids
  • show subjects are sensitive to structure in these inf-bandit, reward-based domains (see RLDM, even though it doesn't use this exp.)

progress so far:

  • run 30 subs in each group
  • structure not perfect like previous fittings; still trying to understand why high and medium volatility group fits look swapped

todo:

  • figure out above struct issue
  • run fits for infomax, pure exploitation, weighted hybrid, and possibly GP-UCB and PI AFs
